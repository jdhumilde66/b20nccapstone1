ABOUT

CC is a gesture rather than a name to identify a group or collective. It began as a gesture to rethink the idea of collaboration amidst community driven engagement. CC, just like in an e-mail, involves others. CC works with others. CC is a gesture to go beyond oneself, it is an extension of ones own art practice, a platform to expand and explore possibilities by contributing, combining, or complementing each one's strengths and weaknesses to come up with projects one can not simply do alone. CC can happen anywhere, CC can be everyone.

TEAM

Anjo Bolarda

Bolarda is an artist, a graphic designer, illustrator, muralist, and a cultural worker whose works has been exhibited in several local and international venues. His practice originates from experiences and observations of the everyday: beliefs, myths, stories, food, habits, including the ways people improvise ordinary objects to create other useful things. He translates these into his own works through paintings, objects, installations, video, events and performances. Bolarda creates projects in which strangers can contemplate, explore and be aware of the actions and actual experiences that makes up our daily life.

Issay Rodriguez

Rodriguez's portfolio of artworks includes installations, drawings, prints, objects, and videos that allude to multiple tactile histories. The heart of her work lies in connecting gaps and fragments of memories, places and identities. Her projects allow her to think about image and object production amidst the daily does of technology mixed with post-modern. She views her work as an extension of one's inward investigations since they are autoethnographic. She embeds narratives extracted from achival research as well as non-digital human interactions. Rodriguez works towards new configurations in the form of the tactile and the sensory (at times participatory) art projects in this age of fascination for the instantaneous but distressing world filled with optical pleasure.
